Building 6402+

6402+ was born from the commitment of many hands and hearts. In this section we share with you the process, the team behind the project and the thanks to those who helped make it possible.

What were the false positives in Colombia?

False positives" were systematic killings of innocent civilians by the Colombian Army, especially between 2002 and 2008. These people, many of them young people from poor neighborhoods or rural areas, were offered false job opportunities, transferred to other regions of the country and then executed. The military then presented their bodies as if they were enemy combatants killed in clashes, to show supposed successes in the war.

This occurred in the context of Colombia's armed conflict, an internal war that for decades has pitted the state against armed insurgent groups, such as the FARC and ELN guerrillas, as well as paramilitaries and drug traffickers. During this conflict, governments sought to show military results before public opinion and the international community. Some sectors of the Army received incentives, such as days off, promotions or awards, for reporting combat casualties.

The “false positives” were not isolated cases. An investigation by the Special Jurisdiction for Peace (JEP), a tribunal created after the peace agreement in Colombia, documented at least 6,402 victims of this state crime.

Behind each number is a life story cut short, and thousands of families who have struggled for years for truth, justice and recognition.

“6402+ | Seeds of memory” is a joint project between Wikimedia Colombia and students of Interactive Design at the Universidad Jorge Tadeo Lozano, created in Colombia with the purpose of dignifying the memory of the victims of false positives. Through a digital proposal, the project seeks to make these stories visible from the humanity of those who lived them and not from the figures. This initiative offers a space for the families of the victims to be the ones to tell and build the memory, redefining the official story that for years has silenced their voices. Through data visualizations, written testimonies and a sensitive narrative, the project contributes to keeping history alive, not as a closed archive, but as an expanding memory. “6402+ | Seeds of Memory” is aimed at victims, family members, researchers, activists and anyone interested in understanding this part of Colombian history from a human, critical and collective perspective.
